# Monate und Zeitangaben - Months and Time Expressions

## Exercise: Wann? (When?)

### Original Sentence (with error):
Ich fahre **in** August mit der Bahn nach Wien.

### ❌ Error: "**in** August"
**Should be:** "**im** August"

**Explanation:**
- When talking about months, we use **im** (= in + dem)
- All months in German are masculine and take the article **der**
- "in" + "dem August" → **im August**

### Corrected Sentence:
Ich fahre **im** August mit der Bahn nach Wien.

**Translation:**
I'm traveling by train to Vienna in August.

---

## Rule: All German Months are Masculine (der)

### The 12 Months - Die 12 Monate:
- **der** Januar → **im** Januar (in January)
- **der** Februar → **im** Februar (in February)
- **der** März → **im** März (in March)
- **der** April → **im** April (in April)
- **der** Mai → **im** Mai (in May)
- **der** Juni → **im** Juni (in June)
- **der** Juli → **im** Juli (in July)
- **der** August → **im** August (in August)
- **der** September → **im** September (in September)
- **der** Oktober → **im** Oktober (in October)
- **der** November → **im** November (in November)
- **der** Dezember → **im** Dezember (in December)

**Key Point:** Always use **im** (never just "in") when talking about months!

---

## Time Prepositions in German

### Months: **im** (in + dem)
- Ich habe **im** Juli Geburtstag. (I have my birthday in July)
- **Im** Dezember schneit es oft. (It often snows in December)

### Seasons: **im** (in + dem)
All seasons except Frühling use "im":
- **im** Frühling / **im** Frühjahr (in spring)
- **im** Sommer (in summer)
- **im** Herbst (in autumn/fall)
- **im** Winter (in winter)

### Years: **im Jahr** or just the year with no preposition
- **im Jahr** 2024 (in the year 2024)
- OR: 2024 reiste ich nach Wien. (In 2024 I traveled to Vienna)

### Days of the week: **am** (an + dem)
- **am** Montag (on Monday)
- **am** Dienstag (on Tuesday)
- **am** Mittwoch (on Wednesday)
- **am** Donnerstag (on Thursday)
- **am** Freitag (on Friday)
- **am** Samstag (on Saturday)
- **am** Sonntag (on Sunday)

### Times of day: **am** (morning/evening), **in der** (night), **um** (clock time)
- **am** Morgen (in the morning)
- **am** Nachmittag (in the afternoon)
- **am** Abend (in the evening)
- **in der** Nacht (at night)
- **um** 8 Uhr (at 8 o'clock)

### Dates: **am** (an + dem)
- **am** 15. August (on August 15th)
- **am** ersten Mai (on May 1st)

## Practice Sentences:

1. Mein Geburtstag ist **im** März.
   (My birthday is in March)

2. Wir fahren **im** Sommer nach Spanien.
   (We're going to Spain in summer)

3. **Am** Montag habe ich einen Termin.
   (I have an appointment on Monday)

4. Der Zug fährt **um** 14:30 Uhr ab.
   (The train departs at 2:30 PM)

5. **Am** 25. Dezember ist Weihnachten.
   (Christmas is on December 25th)

6. Ich arbeite oft **in der** Nacht.
   (I often work at night)

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid:

❌ **in** August → ✅ **im** August
❌ **in** Montag → ✅ **am** Montag
❌ **in** 8 Uhr → ✅ **um** 8 Uhr
❌ **auf** Sommer → ✅ **im** Sommer
