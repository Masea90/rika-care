# Relativpronomen - Relative Pronouns

## Exercise: Complete the sentence

### Sentence:
Warum müssen die Aufgaben, **die** uns unser Lehrer stellt, eigentlich immer so schwierig sein?

**Translation:**
Why do the tasks that our teacher gives us actually always have to be so difficult?

---

## Answer: **die**

### Why "die"?
1. **Antecedent (Bezugswort):** die Aufgaben (plural, feminine)
2. **Case needed:** Akkusativ
   - In the relative clause: "unser Lehrer stellt [die Aufgaben] uns"
   - "die Aufgaben" is the direct object of "stellt"
   - Direct object = Akkusativ
3. **Relative pronoun:** die (plural, Akkusativ)

---

## Relative Pronouns Table - Relativpronomen Tabelle

Relative pronouns must match the **gender and number** of the noun they refer to, but their **case** depends on their function in the relative clause.

### Singular:

| Case | Maskulin (der) | Feminin (die) | Neutrum (das) |
|------|----------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Nominativ** | der | die | das |
| **Akkusativ** | den | die | das |
| **Dativ** | dem | der | dem |
| **Genitiv** | dessen | deren | dessen |

### Plural (all genders):

| Case | Plural (die) |
|------|--------------|
| **Nominativ** | die |
| **Akkusativ** | die |
| **Dativ** | denen |
| **Genitiv** | deren |

---

## How to Choose the Correct Relative Pronoun:

### Step 1: Identify the antecedent (the noun it refers to)
- What is the gender? (der/die/das)
- Is it singular or plural?

### Step 2: Determine the case needed in the relative clause
- **Nominativ:** Subject of the relative clause
- **Akkusativ:** Direct object in the relative clause
- **Dativ:** Indirect object or after Dativ prepositions
- **Genitiv:** Shows possession

### Step 3: Select the appropriate relative pronoun from the table

---

## Examples:

### Example 1: Nominativ (Subject)
**Der Mann, der dort steht, ist mein Lehrer.**
- Antecedent: der Mann (masculine, singular)
- Function: subject in relative clause (who stands)
- Relative pronoun: **der** (masculine, singular, Nominativ)
- Translation: The man who is standing there is my teacher.

### Example 2: Akkusativ (Direct Object)
**Die Aufgaben, die uns unser Lehrer stellt, sind schwierig.**
- Antecedent: die Aufgaben (plural)
- Function: direct object in relative clause (the teacher gives WHAT?)
- Relative pronoun: **die** (plural, Akkusativ)
- Translation: The tasks that our teacher gives us are difficult.

### Example 3: Dativ (Indirect Object)
**Der Freund, dem ich geholfen habe, ist sehr dankbar.**
- Antecedent: der Freund (masculine, singular)
- Function: indirect object (helfen takes Dativ: "ich habe WEM geholfen?")
- Relative pronoun: **dem** (masculine, singular, Dativ)
- Translation: The friend whom I helped is very grateful.

### Example 4: Genitiv (Possession)
**Die Frau, deren Auto gestohlen wurde, ist sehr traurig.**
- Antecedent: die Frau (feminine, singular)
- Function: possession (whose car?)
- Relative pronoun: **deren** (feminine, singular, Genitiv)
- Translation: The woman whose car was stolen is very sad.

### Example 5: After a Preposition
**Das Haus, in dem ich wohne, ist sehr alt.**
- Antecedent: das Haus (neuter, singular)
- Function: after preposition "in" (which takes Dativ)
- Relative pronoun: **dem** (neuter, singular, Dativ)
- Translation: The house in which I live is very old.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid:

### ❌ Mistake 1: Using "das" for everything
**Wrong:** Die Frau, das dort steht...
**Correct:** Die Frau, **die** dort steht...
(Must match gender of antecedent)

### ❌ Mistake 2: Confusing case
**Wrong:** Der Mann, den dort steht... (Akkusativ used for subject)
**Correct:** Der Mann, **der** dort steht... (Nominativ for subject)

### ❌ Mistake 3: Forgetting verb-final word order
**Wrong:** Das Buch, das ich habe gelesen...
**Correct:** Das Buch, **das ich gelesen habe**... (verb goes to the end!)

## Practice Sentences (Fill in the blank):

1. Der Lehrer, _____ uns Deutsch beibringt, ist sehr nett.
   - Answer: **der** (Nominativ - subject)

2. Das Buch, _____ ich gestern gekauft habe, ist interessant.
   - Answer: **das** (Akkusativ - direct object)

3. Die Studenten, _____ ich geholfen habe, waren dankbar.
   - Answer: **denen** (Dativ - helfen takes Dativ)

4. Die Frau, _____ Sohn in Berlin wohnt, besucht ihn oft.
   - Answer: **deren** (Genitiv - whose son)

5. Das Restaurant, in _____ wir gestern waren, war ausgezeichnet.
   - Answer: **dem** (Dativ - after preposition "in")

---

## Key Points to Remember:

✅ Relative pronouns introduce subordinate clauses
✅ They refer back to a noun (antecedent) in the main clause
✅ Gender/number matches the antecedent
✅ Case depends on function in the relative clause
✅ Verb goes to the end of the relative clause
✅ Comma before the relative clause is mandatory in German!
